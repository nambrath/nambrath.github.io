More about me

I'm a third-year PhD student and NSF Graduate Research Fellow at the UC Berkeley Department of Physics. I'm studying heavy-ion physics with Barbara Jacak's research group.

I'm a former Fulbright-Nehru scholar. Through the Fulbright, I spent nine months studying collective neutrino oscillations at TIFR in Mumbai. In addition to the physics, I got to learn Hindi, travel around India, and lay the ground for some life-long international friendships.

I graduated from MIT in 2021, where I majored in physics and mathematics and minored in French. I worked on a few projects supervised by Or Hen, Dien Nguyen, Axel Schmidt, Larry Weinstein, Lindley Winslow, and Katelin Schutz. I also worked closely with Claire Conceison and Catherine Clark to produce an English translation of Fanny Britt's play Hurlevents. See this page for a full list of my undergraduate coursework. See this page for more on my undergraduate teaching experience.
