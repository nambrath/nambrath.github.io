Research

Heavy-ion physics @ ALICE

I work with Prof. Barbara Jacak's group at UC Berkeley on projects for the ALICE collaboration. My current project is a measurement of energy-energy correlators in p-Pb collisions at ALICE. We see modifications to jet substructure in p-Pb collisions relative to p-p, which is quite striking. I showed preliminary results at Hard Probes 2024, where I delivered a talk on various EEC measurements at ALICE. I presented an extension of this p-Pb analysis (differential measurements in event activity, jet momentum, rapidity, etc.) in a talk at Quark Matter 2025.

Before starting the PhD, I worked with the group for about six months prior to my Fulbright on software to streamline calibration for ALICE's Inner Tracking System.

Undergraduate research

Electrons for neutrinos. I spent two years working with Prof. Or Hen's group and the e4nu collaboration. The collaboration uses electron scattering data to improve event generators for neutrino oscillation experiments. During the summer of 2019, I interned at Fermilab through the DOE SULI program and tested neutrino energy reconstruction techniques on GENIE simulations. Over 2020 and 2021, I analyzed electron-deuterium interactions for my undergraduate thesis to verify how well these methods work on the simplest nuclei.

Axion detection. I worked for a semester in 2019 with Prof. Lindley Winslow's group on ABRACADABRA, a cryogenic search for axion cold dark matter. In 2021, I worked with Prof. Katelin Schutz and others on a study of new methods for detecting axions in the galactic halo using radio telescopes.

Backward Angle Neutron Detector. From 2017 to 2018, I worked with the Hen Lab on BAND, a neutron detector at Jefferson Lab in Newport News, VA collecting data to better understand the behavior of nucleons in short-range correlated pairs. I worked on a laser calibration system for the detector, and measured various parameters of its photomultipliers and scintillator bars. I was stationed at Jefferson Lab for the summer of 2018 working on BAND's assembly, installation, and setup.
