Research

Heavy-ion physics @ ALICE

I work with Prof. Barbara Jacak's group at UC Berkeley on projects for the ALICE collaboration. My current project is a measurement of energy-energy correlators in p-Pb collisions at ALICE, and I showed some of my early progress at Quark Matter 2023. Before starting the PhD officially, I worked with the group for about six months prior to my Fulbright. Three of those months were spent onsite at CERN in Geneva. I worked on software to streamline calibration for ALICE's Inner Tracking System, and began a project on signatures of hadronization in jet substructure.

Papers, presentations, and posters:

  • Energy-energy correlator measurements in pp and pPb collisions at 5.02 TeV with ALICE
    Quark Matter poster (2023)

Electrons for neutrinos

I spent two years working with Prof. Or Hen's group on the e4nu collaboration. The collaboration is using electron scattering data to improve event generators and energy reconstruction for neutrino oscillation experiments. During the summer of 2019, I interned at Fermilab through the DOE SULI program and tested reconstruction techniques on GENIE simulations. Over 2020 and 2021, I analyzed electron-deuterium interactions for my undergraduate thesis to verify how well these methods work on the simplest nuclei.

Axion detection

I worked for a semester in 2019 with Prof. Lindley Winslow's group on ABRACADABRA, a cryogenic search for axion cold dark matter. I worked on characterizing parts of the current detector system as well as performing calculations for an upcoming new version of the detector. In 2021, I worked with Prof. Katelin Schutz and others at MIT on a study of new methods for detecting axions in the galactic halo using radio telescopes. I contributed to the modeling of proper motion and other galactic effects.

  • Axion dark matter-induced echo of supernova remnants [arXiv]
    Phys. Rev. D 105, 063007 (2022)

Backward Angle Neutron Detector

From 2017 to 2018, I worked with the Hen Lab on BAND, a neutron detector at Jefferson Lab in Newport News, VA collecting data to better understand the behavior of nucleons in short-range correlated pairs. I worked on developing a laser calibration system for the detector, measuring and calculating relevant parameters of the detector's photomultipliers and scintillator bars, and writing calibration software for data collection. I was also stationed at Jefferson Lab for the summer of 2018 working on BAND's assembly, installation, and setup.
