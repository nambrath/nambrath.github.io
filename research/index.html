Research

Here are some of the projects I've been a part of.

e4nu

I'm working with Professor Or Hen's group on the e4nu collaboration. The collaboration is using electron data to improve energy reconstruction for neutrino oscillation experiments. I was at Fermilab during the summer of 2019 testing reconstruction techniques on GENIE event generator data. Currently, I'm analyzing electron-deuterium interactions to verify how well our methods work, and assessing how promising deuterium-based neutrino detectors might be.

Papers and posters:

  • Electron Beam Energy Reconstruction for Neutrino Oscillation Measurements
    (in preparation)
  • Studying the use of e- data for DUNE energy reconstruction
    Fermilab SULI poster session (2019)

ABRACADABRA

I worked for a semester with Professor Lindley Winslow's group on ABRACADABRA, a cryogenic search for axion cold dark matter. I worked on characterizing parts of the current detector system as well as performing calculations for an upcoming new version of the detector.

BAND

I worked with the Hen Lab on BAND, a neutron detector at Jefferson Lab in Newport News, VA collecting data to better understand the behavior of nucleons in short-range correlated pairs. I worked on developing a laser calibration system for the detector, measuring and calculating relevant parameters of the detector's photomultipliers and scintillator bars, and writing calibration software for data collection. I was also stationed at Jefferson Lab for the summer of 2018 working on BAND's assembly, installation, and setup.
